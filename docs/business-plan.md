# Business Concept: Ring of Elysium Revival

## Overview

This document outlines a potential business model for relaunching Ring of Elysium under an official licensing agreement with Tencent.

## Why Ring of Elysium?

- Unique gameplay: snowboarding, climbing, environmental hazards — distinct from competitors
- Dedicated niche audience (estimated 10k–50k potential active players in EU/NA)
- Fully developed game — no development costs for the licensor
- Low competition in the "realistic BR" niche since PUBG's decline

## Proposed Operating Model

### Option A: Direct Licensing
- **Structure**: Tencent licenses the server binaries and rights to operate
- **Payment**: One-time fee + monthly royalty (% of revenue)
- **Operator**: Independent company (to be formed)

### Option B: Publishing Agreement
- **Structure**: Tencent retains ownership, grants exclusive publishing rights for a region (e.g., EU, CIS)
- **Payment**: Revenue share (e.g., 30/70 or 40/60)
- **Operator**: Acts as official regional publisher

## Revenue Model

| Source | Estimated Potential | Notes |
|--------|---------------------|-------|
| Battle Pass | $5–10/season/user | Core monetization, familiar to players |
| Cosmetic Shop | $5–20/item | Limited, non-p2w |
| Subscription | $5–8/month | Optional, includes exclusive cosmetics |
| Community Funding | Variable | Crowdfunding for server costs (transparent) |

**Projected Server Costs** (based on similar games):
- Hosting: $1k–3k/month (dedicated or cloud)
- Maintenance: Part-time DevOps (volunteer + paid)
- Moderation: Community-driven initially

## Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Low player count | Lean operations, community funding, targeted marketing |
| Legal issues | Operate only under official license; no reverse engineering of server code |
| Technical debt | QuickSilverX is proprietary; rely on Tencent-provided server builds |
| Monetization backlash | Transparency; optional cosmetics; no pay-to-win |
